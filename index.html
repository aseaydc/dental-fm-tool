High-Risk Periodontal Pathogens (Red Complex)

⚠️ Red complex pathogens are strongly associated with progressive attachment loss and systemic inflammation. Emerging mechanistic research has identified P. gingivalis in atherosclerotic plaques (multiple studies) and Alzheimer's brain tissue (gingipain-mediated; 2019 Dominy et al., Science Advances). These represent active areas of investigation with growing mechanistic evidence, not yet established clinical causation.

Salivary & Genetic Diagnostics (Chairside)

Salivary pathogen and biomarker testing provides objective data to stage inflammatory phase, identify high-risk organisms, and guide antimicrobial selection. These are ordered directly by the dental practice.
TestOrderIndicationWhat It Tells You
MyPerioPath (OralDNA) Stage II+ periodontitis, non-responsive to initial therapy, or pre-treatment baseline PCR identification and quantification of 11 periodontal pathogens including red/orange complex. Guides targeted antimicrobial selection.
MyPerioID (OralDNA) Younger patients with aggressive bone loss, family history of early tooth loss IL-1 and IL-6 genetic polymorphisms. Identifies patients who mount exaggerated inflammatory response (2-7x risk increase). Guides intensity and duration of anti-inflammatory support.
Salivary MMP-8 (PerioSafe/aMMP-8) Monitoring active collagen destruction; treatment response tracking Active MMP-8 is the primary collagenase in periodontal destruction. Elevated levels indicate active tissue breakdown even when clinical signs are absent. Use to time re-evaluation.
Salivary IL-1β / TNF-α Staging inflammatory phase; guiding supplement protocol timing Elevated IL-1β = active Th17-driven inflammation (Phase 1 protocol). Declining IL-1β with persistent MMP-8 = resolution failure (intensify Phase 2 SPM support).
Cariscreen (CariVu/ATP Bioluminescence) High caries risk, multiple active lesions, caries management by risk assessment (CAMBRA) Quantifies total bacterial ATP in saliva. Scores >1500 RLU = high caries risk. Guides intensity of remineralization and antimicrobial protocols.

Supplement Recommendations
Split into two categories: supplements within dental scope of practice (dentist prescribes directly for oral conditions) and systemic needs identified by oral findings (refer to appropriate medical provider).
Scope of Practice: In most U.S. states including North Carolina, dentists may prescribe supplements and vitamins directly related to the treatment of a dental or oral condition. Systemic interventions require referral to a licensed medical provider.
🤰 PREGNANCY FLAG ACTIVE: Supplement recommendations have been adjusted for safety. Verify all supplements with the patient's OB/GYN.
